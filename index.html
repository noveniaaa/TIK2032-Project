Fight Club

Synopsis

Fight Club is a 1999 American film directed by David Fincher, and starring Brad Pitt, Edward Norton and Helena Bonham Carter. It is based on the 1996 novel by Chuck Palahniuk. Norton plays the unnamed narrator, who is discontented with his white-collar job. He forms a "fight club" with soap salesman Tyler Durden (Pitt), and becomes embroiled in a relationship with an impoverished but beguilingly attractive woman, Marla Singer (Bonham Carter).

The Prestige

Synopsis

"“The Prestige” is a captivating psychological thriller directed by Christopher Nolan. Released in 2006, it stars Christian Bale and Hugh Jackman as rival stage magicians in Victorian London. The film revolves around their intense feud over a mysterious teleportation trick. As they vie for supremacy, secrets, deception, and obsession intertwine, leading to a thrilling and complex narrative.

Prisoners

Synopsis

Prisoners is a 2013 American thriller film directed by Denis Villeneuve and written by Aaron Guzikowski. The film has an ensemble cast including Jake Gyllenhaal, Hugh Jackman, Viola Davis, Paul Dano, Terrence Howard, Melissa Leo, Maria Bello, and David Dastmalchian. The film follows the abduction of two young girls in Pennsylvania and the subsequent search for the perpetrator by the police. After police arrest a young suspect and release him, the father of one of the daughters takes matters into his own hands.
